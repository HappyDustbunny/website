How to remap keys in Linux

It is easy to change the behaviour of keys in Linux if you have a dead key or want a different layout.

To see the current keymap open a terminal and write xmodmap -pke

Scroll up to find the keycode of the keys you want to remap.

In case you wan to revert later maybe store a copy of your old keymap by using the command xmodmap -pke > oldkeymap


Eksample

To swap the function of the Escape-key and the CapsLock-key just give the following commands

xmodmap -e "keycode 9 = Caps_Lock" and

xmodmap -e "keycode 66 = Escape"

(NOTE: The keycodes may be different for your keyboard! I use a danish layout)

Confirm the changes by writing xmodmap -pke - or just try the keys ;-)

My journey into programming

I started programming on a TI 68 and continued on my brothers ZX Spectrum and my highschools Piccoline computers running Comal 80.

At university I used Fortran 77, a hideous language with a malign sense of humor: "You have an error. Between line 200 and 500. Good luck finding it."

Around 1995 I looked into HTMl and in 1998 I had to learn Visual Basic in order to theach programming at HTX. I was a few weeks more experienced than my pupils. Fun times.

Around 2000 I was introduced to Python and used it for writing small programs illustrating the physics I taught in highschool. The introduction of Python 3 threw me off for a few years

My youngest and school proved incompatible and after teaching them A level math we swithed to programming around 2017. Pyton at first and later Rust. After the first couple of month I was again in the situation of being only a little more experience, but now my advantage was down to hours: anything I grasped Toke took and run with immediatly. I learned a lot.

After Toke started school again I looked into HTML, CSS and JavaScript. I started making a planning tool in order to have a project and it kind of got it's own life. After 1½ year I am still not finished. Making the whole thing in vanilla JS may not be the wisest decision, but I learned a lot by making everything from scratch.
